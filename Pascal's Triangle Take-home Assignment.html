Page 4: The Secret Revealed

Take the first question in the enumeration lecture notes as an example. If you can only take 1 step or 2 steps, the total number of ways to reach each level is as follows.

Take \(8\) as an example. In Pascal's triangle, the three numbers that pass from the lower left corner to the upper right corner are \(1, 4, 3\). They have the following combination meanings:

  1. \(C(5,0)\)
  2. \(C(4,1)\)
  3. \(C(3,2)\)

In this stair climbing problem, we arrange the positions of the two types of moves: "one move two steps" and "one move one step". For example, \(C(5,0)\) means that in \(5\) moves, there are \(0\) moves of "one move two steps", which means all \(5\) moves are "one move one step". On the other hand, \(C(4,1)\) means that in \(4\) moves, there is \(1\) move of "one move two steps", which means that among these \(4\) moves, there is \(1\) move of "one move two steps", and the remaining \(3\) moves are all "one move one step".

Can you draw an enumeration tree for the above two arrangements?
